Anchored by
Matthew 6:34 (ESV)

“Therefore do not be anxious about tomorrow, for tomorrow will be anxious for itself. Sufficient for the day is its own trouble.”

This verse reminds me to stay present: do today’s work well, trust God with what’s next, and stop trying to live in every possible future at once.

How I’m trying to live
A few principles I’m aiming for
Clear over clever
Fancy ideas don’t mean much if they aren’t understandable or useful. I’d rather say something simple and true than impressive and vague.
Discipline over drama
Real change usually comes from quiet, boring consistency—not big moments. I’m learning to show up, even when it doesn’t feel exciting.
Faith over fear
I don’t want anxiety to make my decisions. I want my choices to be shaped by trust, wisdom, and the kind of peace that doesn’t depend on circumstances.
